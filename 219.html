If the
null
value produced by a safe call doesn’t meet your needs, the
Elvis operator
provides an alternative. This operator is a question mark followed by a colon (
?:
),
with no intervening space. It is named for an emoticon of the musician Elvis Presley,
and is also a play on the words “else-if” (which sounds vaguely like “Elvis”).
?
and
:
are used in a number of programming languages as a shorthand for an if-then-else
expression.
