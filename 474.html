Scope Functions
Scope functions
create a temporary scope wherein you can access an object
without using its name.
Scope functions exist only to make your code more concise and readable. They do
not provide any additional abilities.
There are five scope functions:
let()
,
run()
,
with()
,
apply()
, and
also()
. They
are designed to work with a lambda and do not require an
import
. They differ in
the way you access the
context object
, using either
it
or
this
, and in what they
return.
with()
uses a different calling syntax than the others. Here you can see the
differences:
