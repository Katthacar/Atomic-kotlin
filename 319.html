Constructor parameters are accessible inside the
init
section even if they aren’t
marked as properties using
var
or
val
.
Although defined as
val
,
content
is not initialized at the point of definition. In
this case, Kotlin ensures that initialization occurs at one (and only one) point during
construction. Either reassigning
content
or forgetting to initialize it produces an
error message.
The constructor is the combination of the constructor parameter list—initialized
before entering the class body—and the
init
section(s), executed during object
creation.
Kotlin allows multiple
init
sections, which are executed in definition order. How-
ever, in a large and complex class, spreading out the
init
sections may produce
maintenance issues for programmers who are accustomed to a single
init
section.
