When an exception is thrown, the path of execution—the one that can’t be continued—
stops, and the exception object ejects from the current context. Here, it exits the
context of
erroneousCode()
and goes out to the context of
main()
. In this case,
Kotlin only reports the error; the programmer has presumably made a mistake and
must fix the code.
