The Call Stack
We start in
main()
, which calls
fail()
. The
fail()
call is added to the call stack
along with its arguments. Next,
fail()
calls
illegalState()
, which is also added
to the call stack.
When you call a recursive function, each recursive invocation adds a frame to the
call stack. This can easily produce a
StackOverflowError
, which means that your
call stack became too large and exhausted the available memory.
Programmers commonly cause
StackOverflowError
s by forgetting to terminate
the chain of recursive calls—this is
infinite recursion
.:
