It’s tempting to create yet another logging library. This is probably not a good use of
time.
Logging is not as simple as calling library functions—there’s a significant run-time
component. Logging is typically included in the deliverable product, and operations
people must be able to turn logging on and off, dynamically adjust logging levels,
and control the logfiles. For long-running programs such as servers, this last issue is
particularly important because it includes strategies to prevent logfiles from filling
up.
