Although
use()
looks similar to
let()
and
also()
,
use()
does not allow anything
to be returned from its lambda so it prevents expression chaining or producing
results.
Without
use()
,
close()
is not called for any of the scope functions. To use a scope
function and guarantee cleanup, place the scope function inside the
use()
lambda
as in
Blob(7)
.
Blob(8)
and
Blob(9)
show how to explicitly call
close()
, and how
to use
apply()
and
also()
interchangeably.
Blob(10)
uses
apply()
and the result is passed into
use()
, which calls
close()
at
the end of its lambda.
