The ability to use
eq
/
neq
without any dots or parentheses is called
infix notation
.
You can call
infix
functions either in the regular way:
pi.eq(3.14)
, or using infix
notation:
pi eq 3.14
. Both
eq
and
neq
are assertions of truth that also display
the result from the left side of the
eq
/
neq
statement, and an error message if the
expression on the right of the
eq
isn’t equivalent to the left (or
is
equivalent, in the
case of
neq
). This way you see verified results in the source code.
